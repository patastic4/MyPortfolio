LStore database

The goal of this project was to replicate a Lineage-Based Data Store (L-Store) created by our Professor Mohammad Sadoghi. L-Store aims to manage data that is constantly being updated and at the same time analyzes large sets of data, using a lineage-based storage architecture. My team and I produced the architecture of the database from the ground up. We designed pages to store data, special flags to communicate information, how the data was stored to durable memory, a Bufferpool with a LRU policy, and strict 2PL locking. You can read more about this Database and its design here.
[ L-Store: A Real-time OLTP and OLAP System ]

The Sultans of Suggestion

This game genre is a roguelike 2D bullet hell, where the player starts in a dungeon room and will progress through rooms and gain upgrades until they reach the final boss. These upgrades will be represented in card format with both generally positive options as well as other options that are more chaotic and have some negative effect. Mobs that must be slain to progress will spawn in rooms as the player progresses, slowly ascending in difficulty as they approach the boss. For this project, my team and I used primarily Unity scripting along with certain design patterns to help keep our code clean. On the creative side, we pulled some asset templates and also created some orginal pixel art along with a totally original soundtrack!

Credit Card Approval

My team and I proposed a machine learning model to help applicants gauge their credit worthiness before applying for a credit card, helping them avoid an unnecessary credit score drop from a hard pull on their credit report. In this project, we trained four supervised learning models: Logistic Regression, XGBoost(Decision Tree), Multi-layer Perception, and a Support Vector Machine(SVM).
